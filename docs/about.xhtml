About our project

In the Greek version of the Iliad, the word Andros is used broadly wirth reference to both "man" and "husband." Over time, translations have come to translate Andros with regard to Achilles and Patroklus, long theorized to be a romantic relationship, as "man" to preserve heteronormity. By contrast, the same word, Andros is translated as "husband" with regard to the character Menaleus in the context of his wife. The most recent translation of the Iliad, by Emily Wilson, returns to the original ambiguity of the Greek tradition, rejecting the order imposed by centuries of translators. We intend to use digital humanities techniques to demonstrate more readily the contrast between passages where we identified as ambiguous in the Greek and Wilson versions, but are distinctly heteronormative in other translations, namely the Butler translation in this case. We hope that this can be a learning tool for curious minds and scholars alike.
